## What Stage 6C Does NOT Do

- No `design_ready` changes. The `LinkingResult` dataclass has no `design_ready` field.
- No `conductor_spec_missing` clearance. Conductor evidence remains Level 3 route/span
  evidence; the linker does not inspect or modify it.
- No conductor-to-span assignment. The linker produces a pole-level linking status only.
  Which conductor FID applies to which span between which two poles is not determined.
- No conflict detection. The linker checks linking, not consistency. Mismatches between
  field observations and ENWL records (pole type, condition, equipment presence) are not
  evaluated.
- No readiness logic update. The only permitted output is a `LinkingResult` per pole and
  the markdown report.

## Key Findings from the Actual Report

**All 10 poles linked without manual confirmation.** The notes cleanup merged in commit
e22c733 was directly load-bearing: poles 02, 07, and 10 had been placeholder drafts that
would have returned MEDIUM at best from folder-level evidence. Their enriched notes
(containing confirmed ENWL FID, SPN, and support number) gave the linker what it needed

**The 11kV and LV route separation held automatically.** The linker processes each pole
folder independently. Poles 07, 08, 09, and 10 (LV branch) and poles 01–06 (11kV route)
are linked separately with no cross-contamination.

**GPS proximity < 20 m was a real signal but not a primary one.** The 20 m threshold

01 and 03. Neither pole needed it as a primary method, confirming the priority logic is
well-ordered for this dataset.

**Pole 06 returned two direct equipment FIDs.** The transformer (20636886) and HV link
(11835967) both independently assert `fid_polestructure = 53427080`. This is the strongest
single-pole evidence case in P_LOCAL_002 and a useful regression test anchor for future
linker changes.

## Stage 6D Recommendation — Conflict Detection

Linking is now proven for all 10 poles. The next step is to compare what the field evidence
shows against what the ENWL records state, and flag any discrepancies.

Stage 6D should detect:

- **Pole type mismatch**: ENWL records `Terminal` but field photos show an intermediate
  arrangement, or vice versa.
- **Equipment presence conflict**: ENWL records equipment (transformer, switch, ABS) that
  is not visible or confirmed in field photos; or field photos show equipment not recorded
  in ENWL.
- **Support number mismatch**: field plate reading differs from ENWL `support_no`.
- **GPS coordinate discrepancy**: ENWL coordinates and field GPS pin are more than a
  defined threshold apart (recommended: > 30 m triggers a flag).
- **LV way / transformer outfeed conflict**: ENWL records a transformer with 415V outfeed
  but no LV way record is confirmed, or the voltage context in field notes contradicts
  the ENWL record.

Stage 6D must not change `design_ready`. It produces conflict flags only.

**Recommended anchor cases:**

- Pole 05 (ABS / Fault Making Switch): ENWL records `pole_type = Intermediate` but the
  notes confirm associated switching equipment. Useful test for equipment-presence
  consistency checking.
- Pole 06 (transformer): ENWL records `pole_class = Stub Pole`, field photos show an
  H-pole / double-pole arrangement. Useful test for structural classification conflict.
- Pole 01 (terminal): overhead-to-underground transition context — useful test for
  downlead / UG transition presence checking.

## Stage 6E Recommendation — Readiness Logic Update

After conflict detection (6D), Stage 6E updates the `design_ready` and verification flag
logic to consume three-source evidence.

Stage 6E is the only stage permitted to change `design_ready`.

Stage 6E should consider:

- A pole with HIGH linking confidence and no conflict flags from Stage 6D may have its
  identity and equipment verification flags updated (but not cleared without DNO engineering
  data for conductor specification and pole class/strength rating).
- Conductor specification (`conductor_spec_missing`) should only be cleared when the exact
  conductor FID-to-span-to-pole chain is proven — this is a Stage 6D/6E problem, not a
  linking problem.
- The 11kV and LV route contexts must be handled separately in readiness logic.

Stage 6E is out of scope until Stage 6D conflict detection is complete.
