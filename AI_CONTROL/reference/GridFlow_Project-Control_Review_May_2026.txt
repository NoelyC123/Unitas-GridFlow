Project Identity Evolution
Historical Identity (Phase 1-2, Correct Then)
What GridFlow Was:
"A narrow pre-CAD QA and compliance tool for UK electricity network survey-to-design handoffs. Purpose: validate incoming survey data before design/CAD; catch real-world data issues early; act as a structured QA gate between survey and design."
Best framing then:
	•	Internal workflow tool
	•	Consultancy leverage asset
	•	Narrow productivity and QA layer
This was the right identity for Phases 1-2. The project was proving it could ingest controller exports, apply meaningful validation, and surface issues before design.

Current Identity (Phase C Complete, Decision Pending)
What GridFlow Is Now:
GridFlow is a survey-to-design intelligence and engineering workspace platform that:
Core Capabilities Proven:
	•	Ingests raw Trimble/controller CSV exports (multiple coordinate systems: OSGB, Irish Grid, WGS84)
	•	Applies electrical schema parsing (poles, spans, stays, cables, context features)
	•	Normalizes survey connectivity (EX↔PR replacement logic, span sequencing, route chains)
	•	Detects design blockers (missing heights, absent stay evidence, span anomalies, clearance risks)
	•	Visualizes asset lifecycle states (existing, proposed, retained, recovered, replacement, repositioned)
	•	Provides map-based engineering workspace (15 filter types, symbol hierarchy, lifecycle overlays)
	•	Generates design-readiness reports (meta.json, issues.csv, sequenced_route.json, map_data.json)
	•	Produces PDF QA handoff packs
What Makes This More Than "Narrow QA":
	•	Asset Intelligence — Not just coordinate validation, but lifecycle understanding, replacement logic, stay evidence requirements
	•	Engineering Context — Span distance analysis, crossing priorities, angle pole detection, mechanical support validation
	•	Design Workspace — Interactive map with filter/layer controls, popup organization, visual review tools
	•	Evidence Quality — Distinguishes measured vs. inferred vs. missing, flags review requirements
	•	Operational Validation — Real designer feedback integrated, professional terminology adopted
This is no longer a simple "pass/fail validator." It's a professional survey interpretation and design preparation platform.

Strategic Analysis: The Scope Decision
Why This Decision Matters Now
The project has reached a natural inflection point:
Technical Readiness: Phase C proves GridFlow can handle complex map intelligence, lifecycle visualization, and professional display. The foundation is solid.
Validation Evidence: Real operational use (P011, Gordon, Bellsprings) confirms the tool provides value. Designer feedback is positive and integrated.
Data Model Gap: Current 12-field popup vs. required 35-50 field professional survey model is a 4-5x expansion, not incremental polish.
Strategic Clarity: Two comprehensive specifications exist (Phase C Map Review + Survey Data Capture Research) that describe different products at different scope levels.
Investment Question: The next phase requires either:
	•	2-4 weeks (modest expansion, professional QA+Display)
	•	6-12 months (full platform build, 4-5x data model expansion)
This is not a technical decision. This is a product and business decision.

Recommended Path: Hybrid Approach
My strong recommendation:
Phase 1: Do Phase C2/D First (2-4 weeks)
	•	Implement Phase C Map Review UX improvements
	•	Add 10-15 priority fields (12 → 25 field popup)
	•	Prove professional display model works on real jobs
	•	Use GridFlow in real consultancy work for 2-3 months
	•	Collect designer/customer feedback
	•	Track what questions designers ask / what data they need
Phase 2: Commercial Validation (Months 2-3)
	•	Talk to 5-10 potential customers (DNOs, contractors, survey teams)
	•	Ask: "Would you pay for this? How much? What's missing?"
	•	Validate: Do they need full 50-field capture, or is 25-field interpretation enough?
	•	Determine: Are they happy with Field Maps + GridFlow QA, or do they want GridFlow to replace Field Maps?
	•	Gather real evidence before committing to 6-12 month build
Phase 3: Decision Point (Month 4) If customer validation shows strong demand: → Build Stage 4 roadmap with customer input → Create business case (revenue model, pricing, costs) → Commit to 6-12 month product development → Build survey platform properly
If customer validation shows modest interest: → Stay with Phase C2/D professional QA model → Continue incremental improvements based on real use → Use as consultancy leverage / internal tool → Don't expand to full platform without stronger evidence

Why This Hybrid Approach?
✅ Low risk — 2-4 weeks proves professional display works ✅ Real evidence — customer conversations before 6-12 month commitment ✅ Preserves optionality — can go either direction based on validation ✅ No wasted work — Phase C2/D fields are subset of Stage 4 anyway ✅ Efficient — don't build survey platform without customer proof
The biggest mistake would be: ❌ Building Stage 4 (6-12 months) without customer validation ❌ Staying narrow forever when real commercial opportunity exists ❌ Letting survey research work sit unused because of fear of scope expansion
The safe path: ✅ Prove professional display (Phase C2/D, 2-4 weeks) ✅ Validate market demand (customer conversations, 2-3 months) ✅ Then decide Stage 4 based on real evidence
