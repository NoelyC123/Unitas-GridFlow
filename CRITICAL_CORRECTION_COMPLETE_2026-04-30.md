**Problem being corrected:** Earlier wording suggested GridFlow REPLACES Field Maps' basic record display.

**Why this is wrong:** If GridFlow doesn't show records clearly, it's worse than Field Maps, not better.

## NEW (CORRECT) FRAMING

**Field Maps shows:** What records are on the map

**GridFlow shows:**
1. **What records are on the map** (Field Maps parity - MUST have this)
2. **PLUS:** What this survey data means for design
3. **PLUS:** What is missing or incomplete
4. **PLUS:** What remains provisional
5. **PLUS:** What must be checked before CAD/design

**GridFlow = Field Maps functionality PLUS design-readiness interpretation layer**

## Why This Matters

**If GridFlow tries to replace Field Maps:**
- Sets up a competition GridFlow will lose
- Field Maps is mature enterprise GIS
- GridFlow would be compared on record display quality
- We lose

**If GridFlow adds to Field Maps:**
- Creates unique value Field Maps doesn't provide
- Record display parity is table stakes, not differentiator
- Interpretation layer is the unique value
- We win
